# Student Clubs and Organizations at Northfield University

Student life at Northfield University includes more than 80 clubs and organizations, ranging from technical communities to cultural, service, recreational, and creative groups. Students often find their first friendships, leadership opportunities, and project collaborators through campus involvement.

Popular student organizations include the **AI Builders Society**, **Women in Computing**, **Cyber Defense Club**, **Robotics Team**, **Game Development Guild**, **Entrepreneurship Network**, and the **International Student Association**. Most clubs host an interest meeting during the first three weeks of each semester, and many participate in the annual **Northfield Involvement Fair** held on the second Wednesday of the fall term.

Students can browse active organizations through the campus engagement portal, where they can see meeting times, officer contacts, and upcoming events. Joining a club is usually as simple as attending a meeting or clicking “Join” in the portal, though some competition teams require tryouts or project commitments.

If students do not see a club that fits their interests, they may apply to start a new one. To register a new organization, students need at least **five currently enrolled members**, a faculty or staff advisor, a draft constitution, and a completed new organization form submitted by **September 30** for fall approval or **February 15** for spring approval.

The Office of Student Engagement supports club leadership training, event planning, budget requests, and room reservations. Students can contact the office at **clubs@northfield.edu** or visit Student Union West, Room 104, Monday through Friday from **9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.**
