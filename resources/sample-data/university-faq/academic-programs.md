# Academic Programs at Northfield University

Northfield University is a fictional technology-focused institution known for interdisciplinary programs that connect computing, design, business, and responsible innovation. Students can choose from undergraduate majors, graduate programs, and certificate pathways that combine technical depth with practical experience.

Popular **undergraduate majors** include Computer Science, Data Science, Cybersecurity, Information Systems, Human-Centered Design, Robotics Engineering, and Digital Business. Students interested in AI often choose Computer Science with the **Applied AI concentration**, which includes coursework in machine learning, natural language processing, cloud development, and responsible AI practices.

At the **graduate level**, Northfield offers a Master of Science in Artificial Intelligence, a Master of Science in Data Engineering, a Master of Cybersecurity Practice, and a Professional Certificate in Cloud Application Development. Graduate students often complete applied capstone projects in partnership with industry mentors and university research labs.

All undergraduates complete the **Northfield Core**, which includes communication, ethics, quantitative reasoning, and collaborative problem-solving. This helps students connect technical work to real-world impact. Students can also add minors in areas such as Entrepreneurship, Digital Policy, Mathematics, or Cognitive Science.

Students exploring majors should meet with Academic Advising before registering for their second semester. Advising appointments can be booked through the student portal or by emailing **advising@northfield.edu**. Drop-in advising is available in Horizon Center, Room 210, on weekdays from **10:00 a.m. to 2:00 p.m.** Program sheets, sample degree maps, and catalog descriptions are updated each spring for the upcoming academic year.
