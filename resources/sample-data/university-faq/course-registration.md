# Course Registration at Northfield University

Northfield University uses an online registration system that allows students to search for classes, review degree requirements, and build schedules before their assigned registration window opens. Registration times are based on earned credits, so seniors and graduate students typically register before juniors, sophomores, and first-year students.

Before registering, students should review any holds on their account, meet with an advisor if required by their program, and confirm that they have satisfied prerequisites. The registration planning tool lets students save preferred schedules and alternate sections. Students may enroll in up to **18 credits** during a fall or spring semester without special approval. Requests to exceed the standard limit must be approved by Academic Advising.

For the fall 2026 term, registration opens on **April 6** for graduate students and **April 8-15** for undergraduates based on class standing. The **add/drop period** runs through the end of the first week of classes. Students may add courses until **11:59 p.m. on the seventh calendar day** of the semester. Courses dropped during that period are removed without academic penalty. After the add/drop deadline, withdrawal deadlines and refund schedules apply.

Students who are waitlisted should monitor their university email closely, as waitlist offers expire after **24 hours**. Registration help is available through the Registrar’s Office at **registrar@northfield.edu** or **(555) 010-2418**. The registration help desk is staffed Monday through Friday from **8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.** During peak registration weeks, the office also offers live chat support through the student portal from **6:00 p.m. to 8:00 p.m.**
