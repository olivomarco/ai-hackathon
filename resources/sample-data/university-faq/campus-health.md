# Campus Health and Counseling at Northfield University

Northfield University Student Health & Wellness supports students’ physical health, mental health, and overall well-being. Services are designed to help students stay healthy, manage stress, and get connected to care early, especially during transitions, high-pressure academic periods, or personal challenges.

The Student Health Center provides treatment for minor illnesses and injuries, preventive care, immunization review, basic lab services, and referrals to off-campus specialists when needed. The clinic is located in Wellness Center East and is open Monday through Friday from **8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.** Same-day sick visits are often available, but students are encouraged to schedule non-urgent appointments in advance.

Counseling Services offers short-term individual counseling, crisis support, group workshops, and referrals for longer-term care. Common support topics include anxiety, stress, homesickness, burnout, grief, and adjustment to college life. Counselors also lead skill-building sessions on time management, sleep, and coping strategies during midterms and finals.

Students experiencing an urgent mental health concern should call **(555) 010-2777** during business hours or use the after-hours crisis line provided on the wellness portal. In an immediate emergency, students should call 911 or campus safety. To schedule a routine counseling appointment, email **counseling@northfield.edu**. General health questions can be sent to **healthcenter@northfield.edu**.

Northfield encourages students to ask for help early. Seeking support is a sign of strength, and wellness staff regularly coordinate with Accessibility Services, Residence Life, and Academic Advising when students need broader support plans.
