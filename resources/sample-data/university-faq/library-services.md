# Library Services at Northfield University

The Northfield University Library supports coursework, research, and project work across the university. Located in Discovery Center, the library offers quiet study spaces, group collaboration rooms, maker resources, research workshops, and access to digital collections.

During the fall and spring semesters, the main library is open Monday through Thursday from **7:30 a.m. to midnight**, Friday from **7:30 a.m. to 8:00 p.m.**, Saturday from **10:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m.**, and Sunday from **noon to midnight**. Final exam extended hours are announced each term on the library website and student portal.

Students have access to online journals, e-books, streaming media, and research databases from both on campus and off campus. When using library databases remotely, students should sign in with their Northfield credentials through the library portal. Librarians can help with citation questions, database search strategies, literature reviews, and source evaluation.

The Research Help Desk offers walk-in support Monday through Friday from **11:00 a.m. to 4:00 p.m.** Students can also book a virtual or in-person consultation with a subject librarian. Technology lending includes laptops, webcams, chargers, and podcast kits, typically available for 3-day or 7-day checkout depending on demand.

For help with borrowing, reserves, or research services, contact **library@northfield.edu**. Faculty and graduate students who need support with systematic reviews, data management planning, or workshop requests may email **researchsupport@northfield.edu**. The library encourages students to ask for help early, especially when working on major research papers or capstone projects.
