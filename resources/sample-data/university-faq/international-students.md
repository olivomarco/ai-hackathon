# International Student Support at Northfield University

Northfield University welcomes students from around the world and provides advising to help them maintain immigration status, adjust to campus life, and plan for academic success. International students should work closely with the International Student Support team on visa, travel, employment, and enrollment questions.

Most international students at Northfield study in **F-1 status**. Newly admitted students must submit financial documentation and confirm their enrollment before the university can issue the **Form I-20**. Students should review their admission checklist carefully and allow extra time for visa appointments, travel planning, and document delivery. The recommended deadline to submit final paperwork for fall entry is **June 15**.

After arrival, all new international students must complete the mandatory check-in process and attend international orientation. F-1 students are expected to maintain full-time enrollment, keep a valid passport, update their local U.S. address, and consult an advisor before dropping below full-time status. Questions about Curricular Practical Training (CPT), Optional Practical Training (OPT), travel signatures, or reduced course loads should be discussed with an international student advisor before action is taken.

International Student Support also offers workshops on employment rules, cultural adjustment, health insurance, and academic expectations in the U.S. Students can reach the office at **international@northfield.edu** or visit Global Commons, Room 115. Advising hours are Monday through Friday from **9:00 a.m. to 4:00 p.m.**, with virtual appointments available on Tuesdays and Thursdays.
