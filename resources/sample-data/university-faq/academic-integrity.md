# Academic Integrity and Responsible AI Use at Northfield University

Northfield University expects all students to uphold the principles of honesty, trust, fairness, respect, and responsibility in academic work. The university’s Academic Integrity Code applies to exams, homework, lab reports, programming assignments, essays, presentations, and collaborative projects unless an instructor states otherwise.

**Plagiarism**, unauthorized collaboration, fabrication of sources or data, and using prohibited materials during assessments are all violations of the code. Instructors may also define assignment-specific rules about citation, collaboration, drafts, and the use of external tools.

Northfield recognizes that AI tools are now part of many academic and professional workflows. Students may use approved AI tools only when course or assignment instructions allow it. When AI assistance is permitted, students are expected to disclose that use if requested by the instructor and remain responsible for verifying accuracy, citing sources where appropriate, and ensuring the final submission reflects their own understanding.

Students may **not** use AI tools to generate answers for closed-book exams, complete take-home assessments that prohibit outside assistance, fabricate references, or disguise copied work as original writing or code. Claiming AI-generated content as entirely your own when the assignment does not allow it may be treated as an academic integrity violation.

Possible outcomes for violations include assignment penalties, a failing grade, educational sanctions, or referral to the Academic Integrity Board. Students with questions should review their course syllabus first, then contact the Office of Academic Integrity at **integrity@northfield.edu**. The office offers student advising appointments Monday, Wednesday, and Friday from **10:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m.** in Administration Hall, Room 208.
