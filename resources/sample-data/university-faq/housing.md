# Housing and Dining at Northfield University

Northfield University offers a residential experience designed to help students build community quickly, especially during the first year. Most first-year undergraduate students live in one of three residence halls: Maple Hall, Cedar Hall, or Innovation Commons. Returning students may apply for apartment-style housing in Lakeside Village or Technology Court, depending on availability.

Students who want on-campus housing should complete the housing application and submit the enrollment deposit before **May 15** for fall assignments. Room assignments are typically released in the first two weeks of July. Students can indicate roommate preferences, accessibility accommodations, and living-learning community interest through the housing portal. While the university considers preferences carefully, assignment requests are not guaranteed.

Each residence hall includes furnished rooms, wireless internet, laundry facilities, study lounges, and secure entry. Dining plans are required for students living in traditional residence halls. The most popular option is the **Northfield Flex 14** plan, which includes 14 dining hall meals per week plus $250 in café credit each semester. Vegetarian, vegan, halal, and allergen-aware options are available in the main dining hall.

Students needing housing accommodations should contact Accessibility Services and Residence Life as early as possible. Questions about move-in, break housing, guest policies, or meal plan changes can be sent to **housing@northfield.edu**. The Residence Life front desk in Cedar Hall is open Monday through Friday from **9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.**, and the on-call duty staff phone is monitored after hours for urgent residential issues.
