# Financial Aid at Northfield University

Northfield University is committed to helping students make informed decisions about college costs. Financial aid packages may include scholarships, grants, federal or private loans, and student employment opportunities. Students should apply early because some aid programs are limited and awarded on a rolling basis.

To be considered for need-based aid, U.S. citizens and eligible non-citizens should complete the **FAFSA** as soon as it opens. Northfield University’s school code is **041777**. For the 2026-2027 academic year, students should submit the FAFSA by **March 1** for priority consideration. Merit scholarship review for incoming undergraduate students happens automatically through the admissions process, so no separate scholarship application is required for most institutional awards.

Northfield offers several named scholarships for students pursuing high-demand fields such as computer science, cybersecurity, data science, and human-centered design. Renewable merit awards generally require full-time enrollment and a minimum cumulative GPA of **3.0**. Graduate students should review program-specific assistantship and fellowship opportunities through their academic departments.

The university also participates in **Federal Work-Study**, which allows eligible students to work part-time in campus offices, labs, libraries, and student support units. Students interested in campus employment should review available roles through the student portal after receiving their aid offer.

Questions about award letters, verification, loan counseling, or satisfactory academic progress can be directed to **finaid@northfield.edu**. Financial Aid counselors offer in-person and virtual appointments Monday through Thursday from **8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.**, and Friday from **8:30 a.m. to 3:00 p.m.** Students are encouraged to upload requested forms through the secure document portal rather than email attachments.
